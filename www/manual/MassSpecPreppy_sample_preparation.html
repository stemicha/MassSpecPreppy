Sample preparation

How to prepare your samples for MassSpecPreppy.

Introduction

Sample preparation is the key to a successful experiment
  1. If you have a insufficient sample disruption the ID number might be hampered
  2. If DNA is still present in your sample the efficient binding of proteins to the SP3 beads is jeopardized because DNA can bind to the beads as well! In addition, LC-MS columns might be damaged because of the DNA contamination.
  3. Insufficient inactivation of proteases in your sample will lead to uncontrolled protein cleavage. This must be minimized.

To tackle all these issues please follow carefully the protocol below

Cell disruption and protein lysate generation

buffers
buffer composition
1x sample buffer 20mM HEPES, 1% (w/v) SDS, pH8
2x sample buffer 40mM HEPES, 2% (w/v) SDS, pH8
disruption buffer 20mM HEPES, 5% (w/v) SDS, pH8
wash buffer 20mM HEPES, pH8
Benzonase cofactor solution 1M MgCl2
Benzonase dilution buffer 5 mM Tris-HCl pH 7.4, 20 mM NaCl, and 2 mM MgCl2
tips for different samples
  1. Bacterial sample: For a bacterial cell pellet use 16 OD-units (e.g. 16 ml of a culture at OD = 1 should be harvested or 32 ml of a culture at OD = 0.5). This is important to keep the samples in the range of the capacity of the used buffers and to equalize your samples.
  2. Human plasma: Dilute human plasma 1:50 in SDS containing buffer (e.g. 20mM HEPES, 1% (w/v) SDS, pH8) and inactivate it at 95Â°C for 5 minutes
